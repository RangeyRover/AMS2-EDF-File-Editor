1. Background
Under the Queensland Train Manufacturing Program (QTMP), the Department of Transport and Main
Roads (TMR) will build 65 new six-car passenger trains at a purpose-built manufacturing facility at
Torbanlea as part of an expansion program to meet predicted future demand growth (with an option
for a further 15 trains in future). QTMP also includes the construction of a Train Maintenance and
Stabling Facility at Ormeau (Ormeau Facility).
Downer will be delivering the design, construction, testing and commissioning of the new Ormeau
Facility under the QTMP comprising of:
• A train Maintenance Facility for the maintenance of the QTMP fleet; and
• A 20-bay train Stabling Yard; including arrival and departure roads, plus a Train Wash and
Automated Vehicle Inspection System (AVIS).
Control of the Ormeau Facility is split into two main areas:
• The Stabling Yard (including all arrival and departure roads to the Facility) to be operated by
Queensland Rail
o To be fitted with European Train Control System (ETCS) Level 2 delivered by TMR’s
Sector 1 South (Moorooka to Varsity Lakes, M2VL) ETCS Upgrade Project.
• The Maintenance Facility to be operated by Downer
o To be fitted with conventional signalling delivered by Downer
Ormeau Maintenance Facility must be capable of maintaining a full fleet of up to 65 trains in a six-car
formation. It must not, however, preclude the future expansion to service a nine-car formation train as
it is possible that the State’s future transport needs will drive a requirement for longer trains and
therefore, it is expected that the train design is expected to allow for the expansion to nine-cars.
1.1 Document Purpose
The purpose this Concept of Operations (ConOps) is to:
• Outline a concept vision for the proposed operation of the Ormeau Facility to ensure a common
understanding of the roles and responsibilities of - and interaction between – site users Downer
and Queensland Rail across the various elements of the site.
• Detail normal operations of the Ormeau Facility to ensure all required operational functionality can
be delivered.
• Consider abnormal and degraded operations to ensure the Ormeau Facility site layout includes an
appropriate level of flexibility to support resilient and reliable operations.
• Support identification of any new/emerging operations-led requirements; and
• Inform ongoing discussions with interfacing projects, notably mainline connection with the Logan
and Gold Coast Faster Rail (LGCFR) project and rollout of European Train Control Level 2 (ETCS).
This document is intended for use by the State, Queensland Rail and Downer as a reference point for
the operational considerations of the proposed Ormeau Facility. This version has been developed in
alignment with Detailed Design Review (DDR) layout designs for the Ormeau Facility and to provide a
baseline version as input to ongoing development of ETCS Level 2 designs.
The ConOps will continue to be updated as new revisions to reflect significant changes to design or
other key inputs as the project progresses.

1.2 Document Scope
This ConOps describes the proposed operation of the Ormeau Facility in its end state, with all QTMP
and ETCS works delivered – and a geographic scope commensurate with the QTMP project
boundaries. Consideration of any intermediate configuration states or staged delivery of works will be
included in later versions of this document as appropriate.
The document is primarily focused on operations within and interfacing with the area of Queensland
Rail control, in so far as Queensland Rail Rail Traffic Crew will be required to operate within. This
includes operation within the Downer area of control as far as needed to travel to/from the designated
Handover/Handback points only.
1.2.1 Out of Scope
Beyond operations to/from the Handover/Handback points by Queensland Rail Rail Traffic Crew,
operations within the Downer area of control are covered by the Downer document Ormeau Concept
of Operations QTMP-MNT-DOW-SM-RPT-000012 rev C and are not re-prosecuted herein.
This document considers the mainline connection in so far as is needed to support arrival and
departures from the Ormeau Facility – however it does not seek to describe operations on the mainline
in detail. Any modifications to the mainline required to facilitate entry and exit into the Ormeau Facility
in accordance with Section 3.2 of Schedule C1 – Performance Specification (Ormeau Facility). It is
anticipated that Queensland Rail will undertake the detailed design and construction of the Network
Connection Works (Ormeau Facility) and any realignment of the mainline in parallel with the delivery
of the Ormeau Facility by Downer and produce documentation accordingly.
This ConOps also does not seek to re-prosecute specific operational scenarios already developed for
ETCS implementation on Queensland Rail’s SEQ network to date. The existing suite of scenarios -
based on the Shorncliffe pilot line (and Robina Yard where available) - are assumed to be applicable
to the Ormeau context. Any additional operational scenarios developed by the ETCS project specifically
for the yard application will be reflected herein as appropriate when made available.

1.3.1.1 TMR
TMR is the sponsoring organisation for the QTMP project and is responsible for setting the overarching
requirements for QTMP, meanwhile the State, acting through TMR, is responsible for managing the
overall delivery of the QTMP.
The Sector 1 South (M2VL) ETCS Upgrade Project within PRS is responsible for delivering ETCS Level
2 works within the Ormeau Facility, alongside the mainline through the section as part of the Beenleigh
to Varsity Lakes (B2V) ETCS scope of works.
1.3.1.2 Queensland Rail
As operator of the Stabling Yard and connected mainline, Queensland Rail are a key interface.
Queensland Rail will be the Rail Infrastructure Manager (RIM) for the Stabling Yard and all
connecting roads outside of the Maintenance Facility.
Queensland Rail are also the operator of rollingstock being stabled at and maintained out of the
Ormeau Facility.
1.3.1.3 Downer
As the contractor delivering QTMP, Downer will be responsible for the following:
• Design and delivery of the Ormeau Facility (excluding ETCS).
• Maintenance of Maintenance Facility.
• Maintenance of the AVIS.
• Design, manufacture, supply, commissioning and maintenance of the QTMP fleet.
• Ensuring sufficient trains are available for service in line with requirements of the QTMP
Performance Regime.
• RIM for the Maintenance Facility.
• Rail movements within the boundaries of the Maintenance Facility.
1.3.1.4 Qtectic
New Generation Rollingstock (NGR) fleet will also be stabled at Ormeau Stabling Yard. As such,
dedicated facilities will be provided for servicing the NGR including a maintenance office, separate
locker room and a cleaner’s wet and dry store. The Project Company, Qtectic have been contracted
for the build and 30-year maintenance of the 75 NGR trains while Queensland Rail operates the NGR
fleet. It is expected that Qtectic can provision the NGR fleet when it is stabled at Ormeau Stabling Yard.
Additionally, the Train Wash, plus Stabling Yard decanting and potable water filling facilities will be
capable of servicing the NGR fleet.

2. QTMP Project Requirements for Ormeau Facility
2.1 Strategic Context
The Ormeau Facility will fulfill several key functions for operations of the South East Queensland
network, including:
• Maintenance of the new QTMP fleet.
• Provision of additional stabling capacity on the Gold Coast corridor – increasing overall network
stabling capacity in line with increasing passenger service levels and replacing current stabling
capacity at Beenleigh Yard (as impacted by LGCFR works).
• Stabling and servicing for Tunnel Capable trains (including both QTMP and NGR) to resource
future Sector 1 timetabled operations through the Cross River Rail tunnel; and
• Provision of additional stabling capacity for maintenance vehicles as part of a network-wide
strategy.
The resulting facility must therefore support required stabling and maintenance capacity, with a layout
that supports long-term efficient operation of the site, plus a high-capacity arrival and departure
arrangement in line with increasing service levels on the mainline.
2.2 Sponsor Requirements
The high-level QTMP sponsor requirements as relevant to the Ormeau Facility set the context and
direction for this ConOps and are summarised as follows:
• The design of the rollingstock will be technically capable of operating in all sectors of the SEQ
electrified rail network, including Cross River Rail (CRR) tunnel operations which will include ETCS
Level 2 (ETCS L2), Automatic Train Operation (ATO) and Platform Screen Doors. Traction power
should be modifiable to allow for future proofing with respect to providing a pathway to additional
capacity, including enhanced speed/acceleration and/or additional cars.
• Associated infrastructure required to deliver and operate rollingstock, including manufacturing,
maintenance and stabling facilities will be designed to accommodate the Full Fleet and will be
located to enhance operational efficiency.
• The QTMP will support long-term social and economic benefits including future introduction of nine-
car sets and the ability to expand manufacturing capability to meet accelerated delivery rates for
future fleets.
• The Contractor for the QTMP will design, manufacture, supply and maintain the Fleet (including
cleaning/presentation services) and the Option Units, if elected by the State. The Fleet will be
maintained to a high standard to ensure reliable and consistent performance over its design life.
Maintenance regimes should consider and implement smart systems such as self-diagnostics and
reporting to the extent practicable; and
• The QTMP will work in an integrated and collaborative manner with Queensland Rail to facilitate
best practice processes for rollingstock design management, testing and commissioning. This will
include incorporation of key lessons learnt from the NGR project and recent rollingstock
procurements in other Australian jurisdictions.

2.3 Performance Specification
The technical requirements for Ormeau Facility are captured in Schedule C1 – Performance
Specification (Ormeau Facility) (Execution Version) which set the contractual requirements for the
Ormeau Facility.
The following operational requirements from the performance specification set the operational context
for this ConOps and are summarised as follows:
• The Ormeau Facility shall not interfere with adjacent Queensland Rail Limited infrastructure or
facilities during design, construction or operation without permission from Queensland Rail Limited;
• The Ormeau Facility must integrate with the TMR signalling design for the Logan to Gold Coast
Faster Rail Project proven using operational simulation modelling in RailSys to:
o Enable Train Consists to enter and exit the Mainline to and from the Ormeau Facility via a
northern entry, such that they do not impart movement or speed restrictions on following or
preceding trains on the Mainline, assuming:
▪ Train Consists are travelling at a Technical Headway on the Mainline of 120 seconds
from Beenleigh Station to Ormeau Facility in the up direction, and from Ormeau Facility

3.1.2 Principles
The following key principles form the basis of this document:
• The Stabling Yard will have the capacity to stable up to 20 Tunnel Capable trains (QTMP and/or
NGR);
• The Ormeau Facility will accommodate a QTMP train of approximated length of 150m, but not
preclude expansion to a train length of nine-car trains (approximately 225m);
• Overall site operation of the Stabling Yard – including arrivals and departures to/from the mainline
– will be managed by Queensland Rail from the Rail Management Centre (RMC):
o This includes operation of arrival and departure roads; and
o Movements between the Maintenance Facility and Stabling Yard will be undertaken by
Queensland Rail Train Crew.
• The Maintenance Facility will be functionally separate from Stabling Yard and be managed by
Downer within their own, separate control system managed from within the Maintenance Facility
by the Downer Yard Master:
o Downer operates and controls all train movements within the confines of the Maintenance
Facility and their RIM including the Northern and southern Shunt Necks.
• All QTMP rollingstock maintenance activities at the Ormeau Facility (within the Stabling Yard and
the Maintenance Facility) will be undertaken by Downer including cleaning, decanting and graffiti
removal.
All NGR rollingstock maintenance activities at the Ormeau Facility (within the Stabling Yard)
will be undertaken by Qtectic including cleaning and decanting.
• Trains arriving / departing the Ormeau Facility will be driven by Queensland Rail, Rail Traffic Crew,
with handback of the trains for maintenance from Queensland Rail to Downer at designated
Handover / Handback points.
o The same applies for movements between the Stabling Yard and Maintenance Facility and
vice versa.
• Areas inside Train Gates for Stabling Roads and Maintenance Facility as well as the Shunt Necks
are considered a yard environment and a speed of 8km/h applies.
• Areas outside Train Gates are to be considered mainline environment. Speeds limits are to be
applied to support safe operations and achievement of arrival and departure performance
requirements.
• Control of the Arrival and Departure roads and Stabling Yard will be under the ETCS Level 2
system; Downer will employ a conventional signalling solution within the Maintenance Facility (see
Section 3.4.1)
• A single, unelectrified road in the Stabling Yard will be included for stabling and maintenance of
Queensland Rail infrastructure maintenance plant and vehicles. Note that operations of this road
are considered herein as far as impacts train movements and control – detail of operation of this
road is subject to further development and is to be included in future updates to this ConOps.

3.3.3 Train Wash and AVIS
The Train Wash and AVIS are located on an arrival road between the northern connection and Stabling
Yard as per the latest proposed yard layout on the Queensland Rail RIM- see Figure 1 . The automatic
exterior train wash will be utilised by both fleet types and managed by Queensland Rail; the AVIS is for
use with QTMP trains only and will be managed by Downer - non-QTMP units may pass through the
AVIS, however data will not be available.
By having these facilities adjacent to the entry of the Stabling Yard, it will allow the Multiple Units to be
subjected to frequent inspections and cleans prior to the units entering the Stabling Yard. Whilst both
Train Wash and AVIS are bi-directional, it is anticipated trains will be predominantly routed along the
Train Wash / AVIS road following arrival from the Northern Connection.
The train wash is capable of washing 10 multiple units per hour in side wash mode, or 3 multiple units
per hour in full wash mode. Queensland Rail will be responsible for the scheduling of each train to
undergo an external wash plant clean on a regular basis.
QTMP trains are currently assumed to go through the AVIS every 7 days (averaging 10 trains per day).
For further detail on operation of Train Wash and AVIS, see Section 4.6.1.2
3.3.4 Depot to Stabling Road
The Depot to Stabling Road allows movements within the yard to reposition trains between the Stabling
Yard and Maintenance Facility. This move requires an intermediate turnback utilising part of the Depot
to Stabling Road to reverse the train between the turnout to Stabling in the south and the turnout off
the Stabling Arrival Road 1 in the north – see Section 4.2.4 for further detail.
The Depot to Stabling Road is under the Queensland Rail RIM and is adjacent to the Downer RIM
along the Northern Shunt Neck. It is envisaged that the primarily use of this road would be for re-
positioning between the Maintenance Facility and Stabling Yard, however it may be used for routing
trains between the northern connection mainline and Stabling Yard as required
3.3.5 Northern Connection
The northern connection comprises of two arrival and departure roads which merge onto a single
connection to the Up Main, enabling southbound trains to enter directly into the Ormeau Facility from
the north. A crossover within the mainline environment allows departing trains to continue onto the
Down Main northbound towards Beenleigh.
The track layout at the northern connection supports trains moving to / from the mainline directly to any
roads in either the Stabling Yard or Maintenance Facility, else into the Stabling Yard via the Train Wash
/ AVIS road.
Queensland Rail RMC are responsible for all movements routed into the Ormeau Facility except for
the Downer controlled Maintenance Facility.
This control arrangement – along with track layout and signalling – must support the required arrival
and departure frequencies through the Northern Connection in line with the performance requirements
in Schedule C1 – Performance Specification (Ormeau Facility). This includes route setting to and from
the mainline at sufficient speeds such that trains can enter and exit the Mainline via the Northern
Connection without imparting movement or speed restrictions on following or preceding trains on the
Mainline.

3.4.2 ETCS Level 2
ETCS Level 2 is a type of automatic train protection system that continuously calculates a safe
maximum speed for each train. An overview of a reference ETCS system configuration (trackside +
onboard) and illustration of ETCS operations at stabling yards is provided in Appendix B.
With Level 2, digital devices on the trains use radio communication to receive up-to-date information
on track authority, speed restrictions and other operational parameters. Lineside signals are not
required as authority information is presented to the driver on a screen on their desk called the Driver
Machine Interface (DMI). The DMI information is continuously updated as operating parameters
change.
The ETCS Level 2 system has a variety of operational modes for managing routine train operations
and allow for a range of operational conditions with varying degrees of supervision. Predominately
trains will operate in Full Supervision Mode but in order to accommodate specific operating
requirements (including within yard environments), other Modes may be used.
Once rolling stock is fitted with ETCS the onboard equipment must always operate in a Mode including
in areas outside of the ETCS fitted area.

3.4.3 Conventional Signalling
The Downer controlled Maintenance Facility area will be fitted with conventional colour light signalling.
This area will be configured as a Permanent Shunting Area for the ETCS system, meaning that ETCS
fitted rolling stock can enter Shunt Mode at any time when requested via the DMI or ordered by
trackside balise.
3.4.4 Transitions
The ETCS must transition between Modes when moving between the Full Supervision, Shunt Mode,
and On Sight Mode areas described. When transitioning from Full Supervision to On Sight or Shunt
Mode the Rail Traffic Crew must acknowledge the Transition via the DMI. When transitioning between
On Sight Mode and Full Supervision no acknowledgement is required.
The ETCS System is unable to transition between Shunt Mode and Full Supervision Mode so in order
to exit the Downer Controlled area the Rail Traffic Crew must select Staff Responsible Mode before
passing the control boundary.
Movements between the Maintenance Facility and Ormeau Stabling Yard will require an interface and
co-ordination between RMC and Downer’s Yard Master to communicate and plan the most effective
time and direction of movements to meet Queensland Rail network requirements and ensure no impact
to network operations. Trains scheduled for maintenance will normally be allocated within the MTP.
The following sections detail the key transition areas and proposed processes within the Ormeau
Facility. Note - ETCS Transition scenarios are currently under review; therefore, the detail herein is
subject are subject to change upon finalisation of these scenarios.
3.4.4.1 Arrival to Maintenance Facility
A transition from Full Supervision to Shunt Mode is required when entering the conventionally signalled
Downer controlled Maintenance Facility from the RMC controlled ETCS area. The interface between
areas of Downer and Queensland Rail control are denoted by Control Boundary Boards at the transition
points at each end of the Maintenance Facility.
A Permanent Shunting Area will be configured within the conventionally signalled Downer controlled
Maintenance Facility. The transition into Shunt Mode will be triggered by a balise at the Control
Boundary Boards, such that the train can enter the Downer controlled area without the need for a hard
stop. The transition must be acknowledged by the Rail Traffic Crew via the DMI.
On approach to the Maintenance Facility, Movement Authority will be provided to the Control Boundary.
Train speed must be reduced on approach to the maximum yard speed of 8km/h before passing the
Control Boundary Boards. The Rail Traffic Crew must make contact with the Downer Yard Master to
confirm a route is set into the Maintenance Facility and on to the allocated Handover / Handback point.
Once the route is set, the Train Gates will open and the train can proceed to the appropriate Handover
/ Handback location using conventional signals. A route indicator is provided entering the Maintenance
Facility indicating route set to roads 1-8.
The Rail Traffic Crew will position the train at the designated Handover / Handback points and exit the
train “handing back” the train to Downer. From the handback, Downer is responsible for all train
operation. All movements inside the Downer controlled area are performed using Shunt Mode under
the direction of the Downer Yard Master.

• Full Supervision - provides a dynamic speed profile

• Shunt Mode – enforces a configurable ceiling speed

Queensland Rail standards for stabling yards.

4. Normal Operations
4.1 Periods of Operation
Under normal timetabled operations, the Ormeau Facility will primarily receive and dispatch trains that
commence or terminate passenger service at Beenleigh.
Primary activity at the site includes:
• Launch of trains to resource the AM peak;
• Arrival of trains following the AM peak for stabling inter-peak;
• Servicing and maintenance of trains during the inter-peak period;
• Launch of trains to resource the PM Peak;
• Arrival of trains following the PM for overnight stabling; and
• Servicing and maintenance of trains overnight.
Queensland Rail is required to provide Run In (planned arrival times and stabling locations) and Run
Out (planned departure times from stabling locations) information to Downer. This will enable Downer
to plan work for Technicians and Presentation Staff.
Downer is required to provide an initial fleet utilisation report to Queensland Rail at 2:30am daily, with
subsequent updates to be advised ahead of a final utilisation report at 5:00am to allow time for
Queensland Rail to resource train crew accordingly.
Downer is also required to provide an afternoon availability report by 12:00pm daily to communicate
status of multiple units for the afternoon services.
4.2 Arrivals and Departures
For timetabled operations, arrivals and departures will be predominantly via the northern connection.
Southbound trains on the Up Main will enter directly through the northern connection and proceed
either:
• Directly into the Stabling Yard via Arrival Road 1 or 2
• Into the Stabling Yard via Train Wash/AVIS road.
• Directly into the Maintenance Facility.
Conversely, departing trains can all be dispatched from any of these locations. It may be necessary to
stack trains on the Arrival/Departure Roads 1 and 2 to meet maximum departure headways.
Routes in and out of the Ormeau Facility will be set from the RMC. Trains must be able to complete
arrival and departure moves to/from a point within the facility boundary such that no impact to mainline
traffic occurs.
After terminating services and completion of associated checks / sweeps, Queensland Rail, Rail Traffic
Crew will bring trains straight off mainline and into required stabling road.
If a particular train is required to go directly into the Maintenance Facility, the Rail Traffic Crew will take
the train as far as the appropriate Handover / Handback location within the Maintenance Facility. This
would be a pre-determined and scheduled movement, organised in advance with the Downer Yard
Master who will, prior to the train's arrival, in communication with the RMC, allocate a slot for entry into
the Maintenance Facility area of control.

4.6.1.2.1 Train Wash Operation
Wash movements will use Full Supervision Mode with a 10km/h supervised speed.
Before entering the Train Wash, speed must be reduced to a maximum 8km/h if not undertaking a
wash. Train speeds through an active wash are to be limited to a maximum 3km/h.
Marker Boards are to be situated in an appropriate location on approach to the Train Wash to support:
• QTMP trains to be put into Wash Mode by the Rail Traffic Crew (both limiting speed and
preparing the train to go through the wash). It is anticipated that the train will remain in Wash
Mode until the movement through the Wash and AVIS is complete.
• NGR to be driven to local speed boards.
• Trains to stop in consistent locations before entry to the Train Wash and during the train wash
as required.
Requirements for a train to be washed will be determined through the MTP, with wash modes assigned
to each train based on a default setting - which can be configured as either full, side, or no wash - by
Queensland Rail control. Once the Train Wash is activated, Rail Traffic Crew will proceed in
accordance with the coloured-light conditions displayed within and 150m either side of the Train Wash,
linked to the required wash cycle type.
Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) tags on the train will be read by the train wash system to allocate
collected wash data and recordings. An RFID reader is to be placed at a sufficient distance on the
approach to the Train Wash.
Following completion of a wash, trains are to maintain a consistent speed (up to a maximum of 8km/h)
on the short approach to AVIS. It is envisaged that QTMP will remain in Wash Mode, to avoid the need
for multiple mode changes
4.6.1.2.2 AVIS Operation
AVIS will detect QTMP trains and automatically take measurements for QTMP only. Both fleet types
must maintain a reduced speed of 3 - 8km/h through AVIS.
Before entry into AVIS, Movement Authority is required through to a point such that the train will not be
required to stop or alter speed within the AVIS building. If a train is forced to stop before clearing the
equipment or the speed deviates by 10% of the entrance speed, then the collection of maintenance
data will be void.
Therefore, a route must be set by the RMC and a Movement Authority provided through to the allocated
Stabling Road, allowing the train to proceed through the Train Gates and into the Stabling Roads.
Route allocation into Stabling Roads 1 – 4 will allow a train to proceed directly; for roads 5 – 10, the
train will be required to cross to the other side of the fan.

4.6.11 Track Maintenance Vehicle Stabling Moves
Track maintenance vehicles will access the dedicated Stabling Road via Arrival/Departure Roads 1
and 2 to/from the Northern Connection, else via the Southern Holding Road to/from the Southern
Connection.
Track maintenance vehicles are to remain stabled during the peak, with no movements allowed that
may impact other operations within the Stabling Yard. Outside the peak, track maintenance vehicles
are expected to move in and out of the stabling facility when required. It is also expected that track
maintenance vehicles will be maintained in the Stabling Yard where track maintenance crew will also
be provided access to.
This road is to be fitted with ETCS Level 2, consistent with the rest of the Stabling Yard. However, it is
anticipated that much of the on-track equipment accessing the roads will not be enabled with ETCS
onboard equipment. Control and position management of these vehicles must still be able to interact
with the ETCS system.
At present it is assumed that the solution developed for management of unfitted vehicles developed for
the B2V mainline will be applicable to yard also. Further detail will be added to this ConOps as this
solution – currently proposed via a handheld device - is developed.

5. Abnormal and Degraded Operations
In addition to supporting Normal Operations as described in Section 4, the layout of the Ormeau Facility
further needs to provide an appropriate level of redundancy and resilience to support reliable
operations. Notably, it is crucial to avoid any single points of failure that would prevent effective
operation of the yard, else block trains arriving and departing - thus significantly impacting timetabled
operations – in the event of asset failure.
This resilience in the layout includes:
• Multiple arrival and departure roads for the northern connection;
• Provision of a southern and northern connection, should access either be lost.
• Multiple potential routes for repositioning of trains between Stabling Yard and Maintenance Facility.
Note in some of these cases, performance may be impacted such that arrival and departure
frequencies to the Ormeau Facility will be reduced, else an increased number of intermediate shunt
moves may be required to reposition trains, but a basic level of functionality is still supported.
In addition to the track layout, electrical sectioning should also be arranged such that loss of traction
power (either planned for maintenance, else due to failure) to the Maintenance Facility should not
impact operation of either the Stabling Yard or mainline, and vice-versa for all three elements.
5.1 Alternate Routings
5.1.1 Mainline Access to Stabling Yard
With normal operation the majority of trains will enter and exit the Stabling Yard via the northern
connection. The roads between mainline and Stabling Yard have all been designed to support
bidirectional operation. Access from the north mainline has 4 roads:
• Train Wash/AVIS Road.
• Arrival/Departure 1 Road.
• Arrival/Departure 2 Road.
• Depot to Stabling Road.
This design provides redundancy in case any one or two of these roads are unavailable, access
between the northern mainline and Stabling Yard is still available even if the trains operate at reduced
capacity. It is worth noting that there is a fourth road available- Depot to Stabling Road for access
between the mainline and Stabling Yard however it is not intended to be used for accessing the
mainline. Based on its location, if there is a failure that makes the Train Wash/AVIS Road, Arrival
Departure 1 and 2 Roads unavailable, it will also affect access for the Depot to Stabling Road.
In addition to these 3 northern-connection facing roads, access from the south mainline is also available
to the Stabling Yard, should the north mainline be unavailable - e.g. points failure in the northern
connection. Trains may enter and exit the Stabling Yard via the Southern Holding Road and travel on
the Up Main in the southbound direction.
This track design allows access from the mainline to Stabling Roads in both the northbound and
southbound directions from a total of 4 bidirectional roads, providing inherent redundancy for accessing
the Stabling Roads.

6. Maintenance Principles
Downer’s approach for the maintenance and facilities management of the Ormeau Facility is outlined
in QTMP-DHC-DOW-PM-PLN-000016_C O&M Services Management Plan.
Outside of the Maintenance Facility, for the Stabling Yard- including Arrival and Departure roads – is
considered mainline environment, Scheduled Corridor Access System (SCAS) processes will apply for
maintenance access. The number and scope of SCAS boundaries within the Stabling Yard are to be
determined, however it shall be possible to for a SCAS to be taken for maintenance of assets in the
Stabling Yard without impacting operation of the mainline or the Maintenance Facility.
As the Train Wash / AVIS road is considered mainline environment, a SCAS will be required for
maintenance of this equipment to be conducted. The AVIS assets are to be managed by Downer -
access for maintenance works by Downer will occurs under Queensland Rail’s SEMS processes (and
as to be agreed within the Licence Agreement and Safety Interface Agreement documents).
Provision has been made to provide blocking on the Train Wash/ AVIS road without impacting
movements between Arrival/Departure Roads 1 and 2 and the Stabling Yard.
