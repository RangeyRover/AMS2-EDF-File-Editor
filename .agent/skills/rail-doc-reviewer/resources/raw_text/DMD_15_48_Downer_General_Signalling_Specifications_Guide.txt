# Introduction

## Purpose
The purpose of this guide is to define and maintain a consistent set of standards for the design, construction, operation, and maintenance of railway signalling infrastructure and associated systems at Ormeau Depot. These standards aim to,
Ensure safety and reliability of railway operations for rollingstock and personnel.
Provide clarity and uniformity for stakeholders, including engineers, contractors, operators, and regulators, to reduce ambiguity and improve quality outcomes.
Replicate Queensland Rail standards where applicable to ensure consistency with local best practice.
This document serves as a reference for all parties involved in Ormeau Depot, ensuring that the railway network meets the highest standards of safety, efficiency, and durability.

# Process Definitions

## Standards Use
Downer GSS documents are applied in a two-stage process,
- Review the applicable GSS from the document library.
- Review the Version and Amendments Control Document to check for applicable amendments.
Design, verification, operation, maintain, test and commission must all interpret the Downer GSS by using the two-stage process.

## Amendment Process
Where a stakeholder wishes to modify or challenge a Downer GSS the requestor must email the Downer Infrastructure Managers Office (DIMO) (insert email) with the following details,
Name & Company.
Relevant GSS and clause number.
Identified issue.
Proposed amendment.
Required resolution date.
On receipt of the email the DIMO shall assess the request and take one of the following courses of action.
Reject the amendment.
Accept the amendment.
Undertake an engineering risk assessment (required for any engineering change).
In the circumstances when an amendment is required the DIMO shall update the Version and Amendments Control Document.
In the circumstances where an engineering risk assessment is undertaken the DIMO shall publish the risk assessment and associated documentation within the Engineering Amendments folder.
